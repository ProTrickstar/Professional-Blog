Neural Networks

Neural Networks are powerful computational models that mimic the human brain’s neuron structure. They offer fast response, efficient information storage, fault tolerance, and graceful degradation — making them the backbone of intelligent computer systems.

A neural network is inherently parallel: thousands of simple processing units (neurons) operate simultaneously, enabling learning, pattern recognition, and generalization. The implementation follows three major stages:

  • Design — Selecting architecture & activation functions
  • Training — Learning patterns from data
  • Testing — Validating performance on real tasks
